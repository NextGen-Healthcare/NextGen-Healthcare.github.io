How Working Groups Work

Our working groups are member-led communities focused on specific themes within healthcare infrastructure. Each group operates independently while aligned with NGHN's broader mission and values.

Key activities include:

  • Regular virtual and in-person meetings
  • Collaborative projects and research initiatives
  • Knowledge sharing sessions and workshops
  • Position papers and guidance documents
  • Networking events and site visits
  • Partnerships with external organizations

Getting Involved

Joining a working group is straightforward and flexible. You can participate at a level that suits your availability and interests‚Äîfrom attending occasional events to taking on leadership roles.

To get involved:

  • Join NGHN (it's free!)
  • Express interest in one or more working groups
  • Attend an introductory session or event
  • Connect with the working group leads
  • Participate in activities at your own pace

Time Commitment

We understand that everyone has different availability. Working group participation is flexible:

  • Light engagement: 1-2 hours per month (attending events, staying informed)
  • Active participation: 3-5 hours per month (contributing to projects, discussions)
  • Leadership roles: 5-10 hours per month (coordinating activities, managing projects)

There's no pressure to commit to a specific level, join at your own pace and adjust as your circumstances change.

What Members Gain

  • Deep expertise in your area of interest
  • Strong professional relationships with like-minded peers
  • Visibility and credibility in the sector
  • Opportunities to influence sector direction
  • Experience leading collaborative projects
  • Access to exclusive resources and insights
